Alternate Company Equivalent: Starlight can be interpreted as a combination of Supergirl and Starfire from The DCU.
Like Supergirl, she is a blonde Flying Brick with a heart of gold, representing the archetypal heroine who fights for good. She serves as a Distaff Counterpart to the show’s Superman Substitute (Homelander), with her costume in earlier seasons featuring a Minidress of Power that is reminiscent of Supergirl’s classic skirt design, emphasizing her youthful yet powerful persona.
She also draws heavily from Starfire. Both heroes share a cosmic-themed alias, with Starlight’s photokinetic powers mirroring Starfire’s starbolt abilities. They both have glowing eyes when angry or using their powers, and their abilities are deeply tied to their emotional state—Annie’s powers weaken when her confidence is low, just as Starfire’s powers are linked to her feelings. Both characters also endure a dark history of sexual trauma, with Annie being a victim of exploitation by The Seven, similar to Starfire’s traumatic past in various iterations. Moreover, both Annie and Starfire occupy the role of the team’s sensual heroine. Her costume in Season 2 echoes Starfire’s Leotard of Power, further reinforcing the parallels between the two characters.

Angelic Beauty: Starlight's entire image is crafted to evoke the modern depiction of angels—radiant, pure, and virtuous. The Antichrist: Season 4 depicts Firecracker and Ezekiel launching a campaign to demonize Annie, branding her as the Antichrist. Their "evidence" relies on illogical numerology, like the number of letters in her name and superhero alias forming "hidden" sixes, the number often associated with the Number of the Beast. This illogical interpretation serves as their supposed evidence of Annie's evil nature and they have enough people believing them. After Firecracker outing her abortion, things get so bad for Annie that she is branded as a baby killer and has a Satan graffiti written over her mural in the Starlight Foundation building.
Her demonization is continued by Oh Father further in Season 5, he calls her a jezebel possessed by demons.
Anti-Hero: Though initially seen as the most straightforward and morally upright character, Annie begins to lose her idealism in Season 2. As she becomes more cynical and disillusioned, her actions grow increasingly unethical, driven by the realization that playing by the rules won’t defeat Vought or stop Homelander. Her shift reflects her struggle to reconcile her values with the brutal reality she faces. By season 5 she becomes a pragmatic anti-hero.
Arch-Enemy:
While her enmity with Homelander is not as personal as Butcher's, Starlight is the second person Homelander goes after the most. In Season 2, he constantly threatens her and sadistically makes her choose between killing Hughie or lose her own life at his hands. In Season 3, after getting pushed too far by everyone, Homelander decides to tighten his grip on The Seven and Vought, and uses his power to systematically dismantle Starlight's life and crush any dissent. He returns back to his threats, forces Starlight to accept The Deep, her rapist, back onto the team, and to further humiliate and control her, he coerces her into a fake romantic relationship with himself. When he discovers Starlight's involvement in a potential coup with Maeve, he makes it abundantly clear that her defiance will have deadly consequences by brutally murdering her friend, Supersonic, and warns Starlight that if she doesn't comply, Hughie will share his fate. Annie hates him almost as much as Butcher and loses her fear of him as a result.
Season Four brought-in another Arch-Enemy for Annie in the form of Firecracker.
