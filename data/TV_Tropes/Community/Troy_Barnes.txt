Abed can pull him into genre play with a single earnest explanation; Jeff can redirect him with a cool-guy speech; and campus weirdos can recruit him into nonsense by sounding confident. The trope fits because Troy’s gullibility isn’t stupidity for its own sake—it’s a social hunger. He wants to be part of something, and he’s willing to believe whatever helps him feel included. That’s why his best episodes often involve learning skepticism the hard way, then choosing trust anyway—but with better boundaries. Watching Troy slowly figure out when he’s being played (and when he’s choosing to play along because it’s fun) is part of his maturation.
Tender Tears: Troy cries incredibly easily, and is, to quote Abed, "deeply ashamed of the fact."
Token Black Friend: Pierce invokes this trope by pointing out that his life has meaning now that he has a young African-American friend.
Took a Level in Badass: Troy’s badassery becomes more usable over time. Early on, his athletic identity is mostly social capital; later, it becomes competence he can deploy in the show’s many war and game episodes. Paintball arcs and other high-concept conflicts often reveal Troy as someone who can act decisively, handle physical stakes, and commit to danger when his friends need him. The trope fits because it’s not just he’s strong; it’s he becomes more capable in the narrative modes Greendale activates. He learns courage that isn’t performative, and confidence that isn’t dependent on popularity. Even emotionally, Troy becomes badass by being able to face adulthood milestones, admit fears, and still show up. In a sitcom context, that’s a legitimate level-up: Troy becomes tougher in the ways that actually matter.
Took a Level in Dumbass: A mild example, considering he was not bright to begin with. He goes from being a standard Dumb Jock to believing the study room table is a magical force that keeps everyone's books from falling to the floor, among other examples.
Took a Level in Kindness: He evolves from an arrogant Jerk Jock to The Heart of the study group.
